Money is a broad term that refers to anything that is generally accepted as:

  • A unit of account
  • A store of value

Currency is the physical or digital form of money used for transactions. It includes paper notes, coins, and government-backed digital balances like your bank account or mobile wallet.

Currency is issued and regulated by a central authority, like the Nepal Rastra Bank in Nepal or the Federal Reserve in the United States.

üß† Why This Difference Matters

Understanding the difference helps you:

  • Recognize how inflation affects currency more than real money.
  • Decide where to store your wealth during uncertain economic times.
  • Grasp the importance of cryptocurrencies and decentralized finance.

For example, gold and Bitcoin have limited supply, giving them intrinsic or perceived value. Currency, on the other hand, can be printed endlessly, which is why its value can drop due to inflation.
